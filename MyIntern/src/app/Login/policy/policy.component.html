General Objectives:

In response to meet the industry’s demand for technically competent and well-rounded IT graduates, the practicum/OJT program aims to:

1. Provide the students an opportunity to be exposed in an actual work setting and assimilate their academic learning into practical applications.

2. Develop and instill among students the industry-desired values of positive work attitude, good human relations, competency in technical skills that pertain to their fields of study, and the openness to keep on learning to reinvent themselves.

3. Enable the student to gain experience, relate and apply the theories and concepts they learned in school/classrooms to real industrial scenarios and problems.

4. Instill in the students the importance of human relations in the working environment.

General Requirements for OJT:

1. All BSIT, BSCS, BSIS and ACT students who finished the requires units prerequisite to the course must undergo Practicum/OJT (I and/or II), in partial fulfillment of the program.

2. The minimum required number of hours of training per program should be in compliance with the guidelines mentioned in Section 11 of CMO No. 53, Series of 2006.

3. A student is required to submit the following documents as part of the final report at the end of his/her training:

a. Letter of Application with comprehensive resume/profile

b. Letter of Endorsement

c. Letter of Acceptance/Approval from the company

d. Parent/Guardian’s Consent and/or Waiver

e. Daily Attendance Report (DTR)

f. Weekly Accomplishment/Progress Report

g. EMployer/Supervisor Evaluation Forms

h. OJT/Practicum Experience Summary Report (in narrative form, overall review and analysis ofg OJT Experience)

i. Photo Documentation of the actual training

Procedure for the Student Trainees:

1. The student trainee is required to attend the OJT orientation set by the college and submit the necessary documents:

a. Comprehensive Resume

b. 2x2 ID picture (2 pcs.)

2. The student should prepare the required documents addressed to the company concerned, endorsed by the OJT Coordinator/Adviser and the Head of the School of Computer Studies.

3. The student is responsible for following up his/her application

4. Upon acceptance by the company, the student must inform the OJT coordinator by submitting any proof of his acceptance (Letter of Acceptance/Approval from the company).

5. The student submits the waiver form, signed by the parent/s or guardian/s to the faculty coordinator.

6. The student starts at the recommended date. He/She must record his/her daily attendance indicating the log-in/log-out time. teh sueprvisor must sign it daily. The Daily Attendance record must be submitted to the practicum coordinator/adviser on a twice-a-month basis (cut-off period, 15th and 30th) and must be compiled and attached in the OJT Experience Summary Report.

7. The student is required to report to his/her practicum adviser once a week to discuss the problems and some queries.

8. the student is required to make a progress report, on a day-to-day basis, which is to be submitted weekly to the practicum coordinator/adviser

9. The student should at all times keep all documents pertaining to his/her OJT.

10. The student must comply with the general guidelines of the company regarding their own OJT program.

11. The student is required to submit a hardbound of compilation ofg the accomplishment report as one of the final requirements
